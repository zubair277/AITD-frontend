QUERIES

Frequently Asked
Questions

Why is PGP Rise: General Management (Global) a great fit for working professionals looking to advance their careers?

Each aspect of the programme has been designed to help learners stay competitive in a dynamic market. The flexible format accommodates busy schedules while the business challenges and practitioner led classes ensure learners can immediately apply what they learn. Tailored mentorship addresses individual growth areas ensuring leadership readiness. Ultimately, learners are primed to drive change in their chosen profession.

How does PGP Rise: General Management (Global) ensure learners stay ahead of industry trends and innovations?

With 100% industry led classes, learners get cutting-edge insight to industry practices. Immersions to global markets keep learners at the forefront of innovation and shifts in the business landscape. Furthermore, tailored coaching guides learners through market changes. The programme’s dynamic learning methodology ensures learners are prepared for the future of business.

What if an applicant cannot attend a residency?

We would urge all learners to attend the residencies in-person, however, they can join virtually if they are unable to attend.

How much time off will an applicant need for the immersions?

Applicants will need about 11 days of leave throughout the course of the programme.

Who are we looking for?

We are looking for executives who exhibit leadership qualities with a zeal to stay ahead in a competitive business environment.

How many years of experience do applicants need to be admitted?

Applicants need a minimum of 5 years of experience to apply for the programme. In case any applicant has lesser work experience, feel free to reach out to the admissions team who will evaluate their profile and advise accordingly.

Is there an age limit for entry?

There is no age limit for entry. However, the programme is more suitable for candidates with experience of at least 5 years. If they have less work experience, we encourage them to get in touch with the admissions team who will evaluate their profile and advise them accordingly.

Who are we looking for?

We are looking for a candidate who exhibits strong leadership performance and potential, makes the best use of opportunities available, and is motivated to create an impact.

Do we need to write a GMAT, GRE, or CAT to enrol in this programme?

No, this programme doesn’t require GMAT, GRE, or CAT scores.

Can applicants get their profile evaluated?

Feel free to connect with the admissions team and our counsellors to discuss fitment with the programme. Applicants are welcome to send their CV to pgpriseadmissions@mastersunion.org and initiate a conversation with our counsellors for the same.

Are all candidates interviewed?

Post the application process, the shortlisted candidates are invited to an online interview with our admissions panel.

If an application has been unsuccessful, may they reapply in the next round?

No, they cannot reapply in the next round. Unsuccessful applicants can reapply for the next intake, next year. Re-applicants are requested to submit a new online application unless otherwise indicated. Please exercise judgement in deciding if they have made any significant changes in their new profile since their first application, that would make a significant difference to their admissions chances.

How strict are the application deadlines?

Our deadlines are non-negotiable. Applications must be completed and submitted by midnight to be included in the ongoing round. A complete application includes the online form, all supporting documents, references, essays, and payment of the application fees.

How will applicants be graded for the course?

All participants will be graded through a combination of quizzes, exams, group assignments, class engagement, projects, simulations, reflections, etc. The faculty will decide the specific grading matrix for each course.

Can applicants tailor their learning journey at PGP Rise?

We offer 16 electives, empowering students to take charge of their time and construct a relevant programme that is aligned with their interests and career goals. We have new-age, core, and industry concentrations so they can choose to pursue a generalist path toward management or specialise in particular areas by choosing industry concentrations. However, regardless of the path students decide to take during their elective terms, everybody graduates with the same PGP Rise certificate with an opportunity to choose industry concentrations.

Are any of the rounds more or less competitive than others?

Each round is equally competitive. The acceptance ratio is the same for all rounds. Most importantly, applicants should dedicate enough time and thought to their application so that it accurately represents their strengths and talents. However, if scholarships are a priority, we encourage applicants to apply in Round 1.

How should applicants approach the essay section?

The essay section in the application section requires careful consideration and thought. We’re looking for clarity, conciseness, and cohesion in arguments of applicants as well as in the structure of their essay. Most importantly, we encourage them to bring out their most authentic self through these essays to give us the best understanding of their thought process, critical thinking, and communication skills.

Are the fees refunded in case applicants decide to drop out of the programme?

Fees once submitted are not eligible for refunds. Masters’ Union reserves the right to amend this Refund Policy at any time without prior notice.

Can applicants defer my admission offer from Masters' Union to the next year?

Currently, we are not offering the option to defer their admission offer. Those who cannot join the programme in the current year are advised to apply again for the next year.
